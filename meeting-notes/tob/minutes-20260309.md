OCI Bootable Image Specification: Every tool that converts a container into a bootable artifact (ISO, disk image, cloud image) invents its own approach for kernel/initramfs layout, bootloader config, and image metadata. Communities like Universal Blue have resorted to bash scripts and just files to build ISOs from containers. It works, but it's ad-hoc and growing more fragmented by the month.
    * Prior art: The UAPI Group's Boot Loader Specification already standardizes kernel/initrd paths and boot entry discovery. An OCI spec should build on top of this rather than reinvent it. Momentum: Ondřej Budai from Red Hat's Image Builder team presented a working proof of concept at FOSDEM 2026 - container-to-ISO conversion that works across distributions, including Debian. The upstream work is available in the bootc-isos repo and the image-builder-cli project. Colin Walters (bootc maintainer) is aligned on pursuing this as a spec. Multiple community distributions are actively building in this space and would likely participate. What we're proposing: Start a specification effort under OCI, beginning with the narrower use case of container-to-ISO conversion and expanding to broader bootable image definitions over time. We have a working PoC, a draft proposal our team is developing, cross-distribution interest, and engineers ready to contribute: https://github.com/ondrejbudai/bootc-isos
    * this would be proposed as a new spec / project. Would need other partners to bring in, but otherwise OCI could be open to it as a home.
